## What is this?

Over three acts, students analyze real and simulated particle physics data to discover the **Quark-Gluon Plasma** — a state of matter that filled the universe in the first microseconds after the Big Bang, and that we recreate today by smashing gold nuclei together at nearly the speed of light.

No prior Python experience is needed. Every code cell is pre-written and explained.

## About sPHENIX

The instructor's thesis research uses the **sPHENIX detector** at RHIC (Brookhaven National Laboratory) to measure jet substructure in Au+Au collisions — building directly on the PHENIX R_AA measurements in Act 3. sPHENIX took its first Au+Au data in 2023 and is designed to answer: *how* are jets modified by the QGP, not just *that* they are suppressed.
